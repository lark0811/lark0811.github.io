Facts and Photos

Jenna Ortega is an American actress who gained prominence for her roles in television and film. Born on September 27, 2002, in Coachella Valley, California, she began her acting career at a young age.Jenna Ortega first became known for her role as Young Jane in the popular TV series "Jane the Virgin." She further established herself with a lead role in the Disney Channel series "Stuck in the Middle," where she played Harley Diaz, the middle child in a large family. Her performance in "Stuck in the Middle" earned her considerable recognition and several award nominations. Beyond her work on television, Jenna has appeared in various films. Notably, she starred in the horror film "The Babysitter: Killer Queen" and had a role in the family film "Yes Day," starring Jennifer Garner. Jenna Ortega's career took a significant leap forward with her role in the Netflix series "You" where she played Ellie, a savvy teenager living in Los Angeles. This role broadened her appeal and showcased her ability to handle more mature and complex characters. As of 2023, Jenna Ortega's prominence continued to grow, especially with her starring role as Wednesday Addams in the Netflix series "Wednesday," directed by Tim Burton. The show is a supernatural mystery that focuses on Wednesday Addams' years as a student at Nevermore Academy, where she attempts to master her psychic ability, thwart a monstrous killing spree that has terrorized the local town, and solve the supernatural mystery that embroiled her parents 25 years ago. Her performance in "Wednesday" received critical acclaim and solidified her status as a rising star in Hollywood. Jenna Ortega is also known for her work in activism, speaking out on social and political issues, including immigration and children's rights. Her diverse roles and strong performances continue to make her one of the promising young talents in the entertainment industry.
